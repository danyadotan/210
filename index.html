What enterprises do when they're afraid

A NASDAQ-listed enterprise, under real market strain and with no settled plan for how AI should land inside the company, went all-in overnight: every employee — technical or not — handed the power to build their own agents. Framed as empowerment. But when people aren't equipped to know what serves them, and no one tracks which builds are useful or even used, “build your own solution” is just the survival reflex one level up.

The name doesn't matter — that's the point.

This isn't one company's mistake. It's a law of behavior under threat. You can assemble the best models, the most compute, a thousand agents — none of it counts until one human finds one useful, walks over, and makes a single gesture toward it. That gesture is the only thing that was ever real.
